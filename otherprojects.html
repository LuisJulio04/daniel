Divine Lorraine

699 North Broad Street, Philadelphia

The Divine Lorraine Hotel stands at the corner of Broad Street and Fairmount Avenue in North Philadelphia, Pennsylvania. Designed by architect Willis G. Hale and built between 1892 and 1894, the building originally functioned as apartments, housing some of Philadelphia's wealthy residents. In 1900 the building became the Lorraine Hotel when the Metropolitan Hotel Company purchased the apartments. Later it would become the first hotel in Philadelphia to be racially integrated under Father Divine.

Gulls Cove

201 Marin Boulevard, Jersey City

Gull's Cove is a luxury condominium in Jersey City. Residents enjoy a combination of luxury, convenience and oversized modern apartments. With a 24/7 Concierge, beautiful lobby, common outdoor patio, indoor parking, the Hudson-Bergen light rail just outside the front door and the Grove Street Path station a few blocks away, Gull's Cove is a perfect place to live. Also, to the convenience of all residents, there is a doggie daycare center, dry cleaner, and child day care on the first floor of the building.
